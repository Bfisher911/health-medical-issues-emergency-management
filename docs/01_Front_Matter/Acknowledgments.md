# Acknowledgments

A textbook of this scope depends on the knowledge, patience, and generosity of many people beyond its listed authors.

We owe a deep debt to the practitioners and public servants who shared their operational experience, reviewed draft chapters, and pushed back when our descriptions did not match what they had lived through. Emergency management and public health are professions practiced in real time under real pressure, and no academic treatment of them survives contact with that reality without help from people who have actually done the work. We are grateful to the incident commanders, public health nurses, hospital emergency managers, medical examiners, EMS supervisors, behavioral health counselors, and community health workers who gave their time to make this book more honest and more useful.

We are grateful to colleagues in emergency management and public health education programs across the country who reviewed early chapter drafts and provided feedback from the standpoint of classroom use. Their suggestions shaped both the substance and the pedagogy of the final manuscript.

We thank the students who worked through early versions of these materials in courses at [institutions — to be confirmed]. Their questions, confusions, and occasional very good pushback were the most direct form of formative assessment a textbook author can receive.

We acknowledge the researchers whose work underpins every chapter of this book. Emergency management and public health scholarship is a growing and vital field, and the foundational work of establishing what we know, how we know it, and where our knowledge ends has made applied texts like this one possible. The citation placeholders throughout this book point toward that body of work, and readers are encouraged to follow them.

Finally, we thank our families, colleagues, and institutions for the sustained support that a project of this scope requires.
